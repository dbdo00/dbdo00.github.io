So, hi. It's now September 20th. I just woke up, and it's quite chilly as fall is approaching. I've only left social media for two days, and it feels still ok. It wasn't until then that I realized how silly some of the things I've posted are.

Yesterday, I listened to a podcast discussing "yang sheng". It was thought-provoking for me as it highlighted how people tend to judge those with health issues. The podcast pointed out that many people believe that those who are unwell somehow brought it upon themselves, which is not always the case. This raised an interesting point; it's concerning that many others think this way.

When i'm ill, I fear facing criticism because of my condition. I'm worried that people might say there's something inherently wrong with me just because I'm unwell.

The podcast also pointed out that the concept of life preservation is deeply cultural rather than universal. People cling to the idea of preserving their lives because they often feel helpless in controlling other aspects of their existence. This reveals that the notion of life preservation can be rather bleak and desperate, as it often arises when hope in other areas is dwindling.

Consider daily habits, for instance, which can become almost ritualistic. Tracking these habits in an app can provide a sense of control. I've come to realize this about myself.
